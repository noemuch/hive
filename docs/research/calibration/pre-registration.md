## What is this document?

This is a **pre-registration**: a public declaration of research hypotheses, methods, and success criteria **made before data collection begins**. Its purpose is to prevent post-hoc rationalization, p-hacking, and outcome-driven methodology drift.

The principle, drawn from the replication crisis reforms in psychology and medicine (Nosek et al., 2015; Chambers, 2013), is simple: if you declare what you expect to find and how you will measure it *before* you measure it, your findings carry more epistemic weight than findings produced by open-ended data dredging.

This document is committed to git before any calibration grading begins. Any deviations from this plan must be documented explicitly in the final report as either (a) minor refinements that do not affect conclusions, or (b) substantive changes that demote the finding to "exploratory" status.

## Research questions

### RQ1 — Rubric distinguishability (construct validity)

**Question**: Do the 8 HEAR qualitative axes measure 8 distinct constructs, or do they collapse to fewer latent factors?

**Hypothesis H1**: Factor analysis (PCA followed by EFA with varimax rotation) on the grader scores will reveal **8 distinguishable components**, with each axis loading predominantly (>0.5) on one and only one factor.

**Null hypothesis H0**: Fewer than 8 distinguishable components are recovered, or axes load on multiple factors in ways that suggest redundancy.

### RQ2 — Human–LLM agreement on rubric application

**Question**: How well does Claude Opus 4.6 (as an expert grader) agree with a human expert grader on the 8 HEAR axes?

**Hypothesis H2a**: Cohen's κ per axis between Opus and the human grader will be **≥ 0.6** ("substantial agreement" per Landis & Koch 1977) on at least **5 of 8 axes**.

**Hypothesis H2b**: Krippendorff's α per axis will be **≥ 0.667** (the standard threshold for "tentative conclusions allowed") on at least **5 of 8 axes**.

**Null hypothesis**: Agreement falls below these thresholds on more than 3 axes, indicating either rubric ambiguity or fundamental divergence between human and LLM judgment.

### RQ3 — Distribution of the three grading actions

**Question**: How often does the human grader confirm, adjust, or mark-not-gradable Opus's pre-grades?

**Hypothesis H3**: The distribution will be approximately **70% confirm, 25% adjust, 5% not gradable**. This reflects the expected balance where Opus is broadly competent but misses enough cases to justify the human-in-the-loop overhead.

**Exploratory**: We will examine whether the adjustment rate differs systematically by axis. We expect *Self-Awareness & Calibration* and *Persona Coherence* to have the highest adjustment rates because Opus, as an LLM, may have systematic blind spots on these axes.

### RQ4 — Rubric stability under pre-registration

**Question**: Will the rubric survive contact with real grading data without requiring major revisions?

**Hypothesis H4**: At most **2 of 8 axes** will require behavioral anchor refinement after V1 grading. No axis will be removed or fundamentally redefined.

**Null hypothesis**: More than 2 axes require major revision, indicating the theoretical derivation was insufficiently grounded in observables.

## Methods (planned)

### Calibration set

- **Size**: 50 synthetic artifacts
- **Types**: distributed across 6 artifact types (decision, spec, PR, ticket, component, document)
- **Quality range**: intentionally diverse, spanning from deliberately poor (target scores 1–3 on specific axes) to deliberately excellent (target scores 8–10)
- **Generation**: all items generated by Claude Opus 4.6 using the generation prompts documented in `calibration-generation-plan.md`
- **Anonymization**: N/A — items are synthetic by construction

### Graders

- **Grader 1 (pre-grader)**: Claude Opus 4.6 via the Anthropic API, using the expert grader prompt in `grader-prompt-opus.md`, temperature 0
- **Grader 2 (human anchor)**: Noé Chagué, Design Engineer at Finary and co-founder of Lyse, project lead on Hive. Human grading performed via the CLI tool at `scripts/hear/review.ts`, in supervised sessions per the grading protocol.

### Protocol

Human-in-the-loop grading, specified in `grading-protocol.md`:

1. Opus grades all 50 items first, producing initial scores
2. Human reviews each item and either confirms, adjusts, or marks not-gradable
3. Human's final grades are authoritative
4. Inter-rater agreement is computed between Opus's initial grades and human's final grades

### Statistical analyses (pre-specified)

The following analyses will be performed regardless of results. Any deviations will be documented.

1. **Inter-rater reliability**: Cohen's κ, Krippendorff's α, ICC, Pearson r, and mean absolute difference, computed per axis.

2. **Factor analysis**: PCA on the 8 × 50 score matrix (8 axes, 50 items). Eigenvalue-greater-than-1 rule to determine the number of components. EFA with varimax rotation for interpretation.

3. **Action distribution**: proportion of confirm / adjust / not-gradable actions, per axis and overall.

4. **Axis-level bias checks** (exploratory): examine whether Opus's scores are systematically higher or lower than human scores per axis. A systematic bias of >0.5 points on average is flagged.

5. **Item difficulty analysis** (exploratory): items where all axes are close to the mid-range (4–6) are flagged as "low-discrimination" and candidates for removal from the calibration set.

### Success criteria (binding)

V1 calibration is considered **successful** if **all four** of the following hold:

- **S1**: At least 30 of 50 items pass the disagreement resolution phase (i.e., are not dropped from the set).
- **S2**: Cohen's κ ≥ 0.6 on at least 5 of 8 axes.
- **S3**: Factor analysis recovers 6 or more distinguishable components (not necessarily 8 in V1).
- **S4**: The human grader affirms in writing that the protocol was followed and the grades reflect their independent judgment.

If any of these fails, the V1 calibration set is considered **provisional** and documented as such. The methodology paper will report the actual values and explain any shortfalls honestly.

## Allowed deviations

The following deviations from this plan are allowed without demoting findings to "exploratory":

- **Minor rubric clarifications** during grading if an axis definition is ambiguous — provided the clarification is documented and both graders re-grade affected items
- **Dropping items** from the calibration set if disagreement resolution concludes the item is genuinely ambiguous
- **Extending the session schedule** if the grader needs more time than planned (quality over speed)
- **Adjusting the CLI tool** during the session if usability issues emerge

The following deviations **would demote findings to "exploratory"**:

- Changing the hypotheses after seeing results
- Changing the success thresholds after seeing results
- Adding statistical analyses not listed here, if their results are then reported as confirmatory
- Removing data points without documenting the reason
- Running the analysis multiple times with different parameters and reporting only the best result
